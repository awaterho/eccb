ALFONSO VALENCIA
 http://www.pdg.cnb.uam.es/
 avalencia@cnb.uam.es
   
Alfonso Valencia received his PhD in Biochemistry at the Autonoma Univeristy of Madrid.

He continued his academic research completing posdoctoral research at the American Red Cross (Bethesda) and EMBL-Heidelberg.

He has been developing computational methods to study molecular evolution and function.

His scientific activity includes the analyses and comparison of genomes, prediction of protein structure and function, analyses of protein interactions and the extarction of information from scientific text.

Currently he is a research professor in the National Center for Biotecnology (CNB), the group leader at the Protein Design Group, the Director of the National Institute for Bioinformatics, and senior Scientist at the Centre for Astrobiology (CAB-INTA-CSIC).

Dr. Valencia is a founder member of the boards of directors of the ICSB (Int.Society for computational biology),part of the steering group for the European Group for Computational Biology (ECCB) and coordinator of the spanish network for bioinformatics.
