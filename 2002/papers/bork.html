Comparative analysis of protein interaction networks
Peer Bork, EMBL, Heidelberg, Germany
Recent advances in proteomics and computional biology have lead to a flood of protein interaction data and resulting interaction networks (e.g. [1]). Here I first analyse the status and quality of parts lists (genes and proteins), then comparatively assess large-scale protein interaction data [2] and finally try to identify biological meaningful units (e.g. pathway, cellular proces) within interaction networks that are derived from the conservation of gene neighborhood [3]. Possible extensions of gene neighborhood analysis to eukaryotes [4] will be discussed.
