ECCB is a multi-disciplinary conference that bridges the fields of computer science, mathematics, physics, chemistry and biology by bringing together involved scientists from all over the world.
Papers reporting on cutting-edge research in all areas of computational biology are sought.
Two types of papers may be submitted, long or short ones.
The selection process will be different for each :

  • Long papers (the length, excluding cover page and bibliography, should not exceed 10 pages) should report on achieved original research (theoretical and / or experimental) or be surveys of important recent results / directions. Selected long papers will be published as a supplement to the Journal Bioinformatics (Oxford University Press) of the conference and be presented in a 25 minutes talk.
  • Short papers (the length, excluding bibliography, should not exceed 2 pages) may report on possibly partial results. Selected short papers will appear in the Posters Proceedings and will be presented in a 15 minutes talk.

The idea behind the short paper submission is to encourage researchers with interesting but possibly still incomplete results, or biologists who usually prefer to submit directly to biological journals, to submit nevertheless their work and, if selected, to present it. Besides the invited talks by biologists, this will increase the number of biological talks which are highly motivating to all and further encourage exciting biologists or bio-analysts to get closer to computer science and mathematics.
Keeping long papers in a separate submission and selection track will allow to simultaneously preserve the high quality of the papers traditionally submitted and accepted to Computational Biology conferences. It is important to observe that the existence of a short paper track should in no way discourage biologists from presenting their results as long papers that, if selected, would then appear in the Proceedings of the conference.

NOTIFICATION
All submissions will be reviewed by several members of the ECCB'2003 Program Committee.
Authors will be notified of acceptance or rejection by email on May 18, 2003.
A final copy of each accepted paper will be required subsequently (deadline for final paper : June 9, 2003).
An author of each accepted paper (long or short) is expected to attend the conference and present the paper; otherwise alternative arrangements should be made to have the paper presented.
All submitted papers, long and short, will be automatically considered as candidates for the Call for Posters unless, upon receiving the notification of acceptance or rejection of their paper, the authors specifically choose not to submit it as a poster. This information will be recalled in the paper notification email.
Those wishing to send a poster only will of course be able to do so. The Call for Posters is now open (deadline for submission : June 1st, 2003). The 20 best posters will be invited to make a flash presentation of 5 minutes.
